Hué, the former Imperial capital

the specification

Hue is a city located in the center of Vietnam, it is famous for having been the former imperial capital of the country. The Imperial City of Hue is a remarkable example of traditional Vietnamese architecture, with elements influenced by Chinese culture. in English

Perfume River: Hue is crossed by the Perfume River, which adds to the natural beauty of the city. Its banks are lined with temple, gardens and royal tombs, giving a quiet, peaceful look to the area.

The buildings are built in wood and brick, they are richly decorated with sculptures, ceramics and complex patterns.

Hue is a popular tourist destination that attracts travelers from around the world due to its rich historical and cultural heritage. It is a witness of the imperial history of Vietnam and is a must for those who wish to explore the soul of Vietnamese culture.

The history of the place

Hue was the imperial capital of Vietnam under the Nguyễn dynasty, which ruled from 1802 to 1945. During this period, the city was the political, cultural and religious center of Vietnam. It housed the Imperial City, a vast complex of palaces, temples, pavilions and defensive walls, which was the seat of imperial power.

In 1993 The Imperial City was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its historical importance The Royal Tombs: Hue’s surroundings are dotted with royal tombs of the Nguyễn Dynasty, each a masterpiece of architecture and art. Royal tombs are important sites for understanding Vietnamese history.

The highlight of Hue’s imperial heritage is the Imperial City, also known as the "Forbidden Purple City." It is surrounded by massive walls and moats, reminiscent of the imperial complex of China. Inside these walls you will find beautifully preserved pavilions, courtyards, temples and palaces.

Its cultural significance

The city is also known for its traditional festivals, delicious food and local customs. Hue is also the cradle of many Vietnamese cultural traditions, including imperial court music, Vietnamese classical dance, and the art of imperial cuisine.

It can also serve as an example of the different Chinese influences still present in the current Vietnamese culture. Indeed its architecture, testifies to the long history between these two countries.

Ao Dai is the traditional Vietnamese garment for women. Each year, Hue organizes an Ao Dai festival that highlights the beauty and elegance of this iconic outfit.

Com Hen is a cooked rice dish served with small clams, aromatic herbs, peanuts and a lime sauce. It is a delicious and refreshing dish.

Banh Beo are small rice pancakes served in cups. They are garnished with dried shrimp, green onions, and are often drizzled with fish sauce. This is a popular specialty for snacks in Hue.

Nem Lui are ground pork skewers that are grilled and served with rice leaves, fresh vegetables and herbs. You roll them in fresh rice leaves and dip them in a delicious sauce.
