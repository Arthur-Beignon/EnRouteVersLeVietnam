    Hoi An Ancient Town

    In Day

    Hoi An Old Town is one of Vietnam’s most iconic destinations. Having this specification that she has two very different lives, day and night.

    The old town of Hoi An is a must-see destination for travelers seeking authenticity, charm and history. Nestled on the peaceful banks of the Thu Bon River, this city has managed to preserve its unique architectural and cultural heritage, away from the tumultuous history of Vietnam.

    Give it its unique appearance, but also its special charm. Indeed you will have the feeling to travel in time, you will walk in the time of medieval Asia.

    Its cobbled streets are lined with yellow wooden houses and century-old shops, creating an atmosphere where time seems to have stopped.

    The architecture is a harmonious blend of Chinese and Vietnamese influences. And many more, testifying to the commercial past of the city of Hoi an.

    In Night

    Although the old town is relatively quiet during the day, it comes alive at night. The night thus creating an ideal setting to stroll, dine in outdoor restaurants, and generally, enjoy all the different activities available last the night.

    At night, Hoi An turns into a picture of fairy light. Thousands of multicolored paper lanterns light up, creating an enchanting atmosphere. The old town is famous for its lantern festival, where visitors can soak up the beauty of this ancient tradition.

    The Hoi An Lantern Festival takes place on the 14th day of the lunar calendar, when the full moon is at its peak. Exact dates vary every month, so it is recommended to check the Vietnamese lunar calendar for if you want to experience this unique and beautiful event.

    The lantern festival is punctuated by various ceremonies and activities. One of the most popular traditions of this celebration, is to drop paper lanterns into the river, creating an impressive visual spectacle.

    the history of the place

    Located on the central coast of Vietnam, the old town of Hoi An is a historical treasure that tells the fascinating history of this region.

    Hoi An has a rich and diverse history that dates back more than a thousand years. Over the centuries, this small port town has flourished as a crucial trading hub on the Maritime Silk Road. It was the meeting point of merchants from China, Japan, India, and other parts of Asia, as well as European merchants, especially during the 16th and 17th centuries.

    This period of exchange has left a deep imprint on Hoi An. Its unique architecture is a living testimony to this cultural diversity. You will find traditional Vietnamese houses, but also Chinese mansions, Japanese temples, and French colonial houses. This fusion of architecture and cultural influences makes it a unique place.

    One of the most emblematic historical sites of Hoi An is its ancient port. Once one of the busiest ports in Southeast Asia, it was a thriving trading centre. The well-preserved wooden warehouses along the river bear witness to this period of intense activity.

    Its cultural significance

    The old town of Hoi An is the scene of many traditional festivities. As mentioned earlier the monthly "lantern festival" is an example of Vietnamese cutlure.

    It can also serve as an example of the different Chinese influences still present in the current Vietnamese culture. Indeed its architecture, testifies to the long history between these two countries.

    Ao Dai is the traditional Vietnamese garment for women. Each year, Hue organizes an Ao Dai festival that highlights the beauty and elegance of this iconic outfit.

    Hoi An is renowned for its cuisine, which reflects both the history of the city and its geographical location, at the crossroads of several cultures. Here is a list, not exostive, of different typical recipes of hoi an the most famous;

    1. Cao Lau: Cao Lau is a dark brown rice noodle dish with thin slices of pork, shrimp, crispy rice croutons and fresh herbs. The peculiarity of Cao Lau lies in the use of special Hoi An water, drawn from local wells, which gives the noodles their unique texture.

    2. Banh Mi: Banh Mi is the Vietnamese sandwich par excellence. In Hoi An, it is often served with a variety of toppings, including grilled meat, fresh vegetables, herbs and sauces. The result is a mix of flavors and textures in a crunchy bread.

    3. Com Ga Hoi An: Com Ga is a chicken rice dish that is a speciality of Hoi An. It consists of tender chicken rice, fresh vegetables and a mint and chili sauce, offering a perfect balance between sweetness and piquancy.
